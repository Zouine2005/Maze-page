A maze game is a type of puzzle where players navigate through a complex network of passages or paths, often with the goal of reaching a specific destination or solving a puzzle. These games challenge players to use logic, spatial awareness, and problem-solving skills to find their way through the maze.

Maze games can take many forms, from simple 2D puzzles to elaborate 3D environments. They are often found in video games, where players control a character or avatar as they explore the maze. Some maze games focus purely on navigation, while others incorporate elements of adventure, strategy, or even horror.

One of the defining features of maze games is the sense of mystery and exploration they provide. Players must carefully study the layout of the maze, search for clues or hidden passages, and make strategic decisions to progress. The challenge lies in overcoming obstacles, avoiding dead ends, and ultimately reaching the goal.
